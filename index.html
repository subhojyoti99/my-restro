Deep Dive: The Book Caf√© (Plan B)

This plan is our most complex and expensive. It requires careful consideration of partnerships, location, and the detailed budget. These tabs explore the key components.

Forming a Partnership

To fund Plan B (or even D & E), we'll likely need partners. This is a business marriage with pros and cons.

The Pros

  • More Capital: If 4 of us pool ‚Çπ1.5L each, we have ‚Çπ6L. This makes Plan B possible.
  • Shared Workload: One of us can manage the counter, one handles accounts, one handles marketing.

The Cons

  • Disagreements: We might fight over money, work hours, or business decisions.
  • Risking Friendships: A failed business could destroy our friendships.

CRITICAL SOLUTION

We **MUST** hire a lawyer and draft a legal **Partnership Agreement** *before* we spend any money. This document must clearly state all our roles, investments, profit splits, and exit clauses.
