Partnership with Friends

The Pros

  • More Capital: 4 partners @ ₹1L each = ₹4,00,000. This makes the plan possible.
  • Shared Workload: One handles the counter, one handles accounts, one handles marketing.

The Cons

  • Disagreements: Money, work hours, and business decisions can cause fights.
  • Risking Friendships: Failed businesses are the fastest way to destroy friendships.

CRITICAL SOLUTION

You **MUST** hire a lawyer and draft a legal **Partnership Agreement** *before* spending any money. This document must clearly state all roles, investments, profit splits, and exit clauses.

Location Analysis ("Place")

For a "book café," location is everything. You need high footfall from students and young professionals. Here are your best options.

1. South Kolkata (Student Hub)

Specific Areas: Jadavpur (near 8B), Golpark, Southern Avenue, Ballygunge.
Why This Area? Massive student footfall (J.U., Presidency). High demand for hangout spots.
Est. Rent: ₹25,000 - ₹50,000+ / month

2. IT & Corporate Hub

Specific Areas: Salt Lake (Sector V), New Town (Action Area 1 & 2).
Why This Area? Thousands of young office workers with disposable income. High demand for "work-friendly" cafés.
Est. Rent: ₹30,000 - ₹60,000+ / month

3. Central Commercial (High Risk)

Specific Areas: Park Street Area, College Street.
Why This Area? Park Street rent is prohibitive. College Street is traditional and may not suit a modern café.
Est. Rent: Extremely High

**Conclusion on Location:** Your best bet is to target the **Jadavpur-Golpark-Ballygunge belt** for students or **New Town/Salt Lake** for the office crowd.

Summary & Recommendations

1.

2.

Your **"Book Café" (Option B) idea is excellent, but it is a much larger project requiring a minimum of ₹5 Lakh - ₹6 Lakh** to start correctly.

3.

To pursue Option B, you **must** consider partners to pool the necessary capital.

4.

Your first step for Option B is *not* finding a shop. It is:

  • Forming your partner group.
  • Having the serious conversation about roles and money.
  • Drafting a legal Partnership Agreement.
